Week 01 Notes Master Mobile UX

Lesson 1

      • Design for the smallest and least powerful first, then content and functionality.
      • Personal computers have been on the decline since 2011, meanwhile mobile usage has skyrocketed
      • Average person has 2-3 devices per person, per household
      • Eric Schmidt: do mobile first from 2012
      • Responsive retrofitting: No touch interaction, goes from big screen to small
      • Mobile first: Small screen to large, provides best experience across many devices, future friendly

Lesson 2

      • Be immediate: if the site isn’t available for mobile, customer will be lost
      • Be relevant: needs to serve their needs, only 9% will stay
      • Be quick: reduce cognitive load of user.
      • Reduce the amount of steps users need to do
      • Reachability: 75% of people rely on their thumb. Reachability and buttons matter. Keep key items near the bottom of the screen
      • Google: ½ second delay = 20% drop in traffic
      • Bing: 2 second delay = 4% drop in revenue
      • Aol: Fastest users stay 50% longer
      • Faster responses keep users engaged
      • 3G mobile speeds are the majority of the global market at nearly 25% compared to 4G at 1.8%, design for less loading
      • Lower image quality and usage
      • SVG/Webfonts are lower bandwidth
      • Optimize and minify CSS and JS files
      • GZIP files to eliminate unnecessary data

Lesson 3

      • Menu and Nav should be at bottom
      • Facebook moved menu towards bottom and the user perception thought the app was faster
      • Menu with a border had 20% increase in clicks compared to hamburger menu

Lesson 4

      • Use placeholder text inside labels to reduce the need of outside labels for forms or float labels
      • Only ask for information needed
      • Enable auto complete on forms to prevent errors
      • Show password by default, and allow option to hide
      • Use correct keyboards for applications, such as numbers only for telephone
      • Use hyperlink to allow users to tap and make a phone call

Lesson 5

      • Don’t have popups to restrict content, loses customers
      • Ads shouldn’t take more than 40% of the screen, start with small one that users can tap to enlarge.
      • Content should come first

Key Points

      • Design for mobile first, then scale up to desktop
      • Mobile is the largest and growing factor within consumer technology
      • Optimize website by reducing unnecessary CSS and JS, reducing the number of images, and GZIP files for data
      • Forms should use floating labels along with only asking for the required information
      • Ads shouldn’t take priority over the content space on a mobile site
